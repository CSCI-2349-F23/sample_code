 Dary's burrowing snake (Adelphicos daryi) is a species of snake in the family Colubridae. The species is native to Guatemala. The specific name, daryi, is in honor of Guatemalan biologist Mario Dary Rivera (1928-1981). A. daryi is a member of the veraepacis species group. A. daryi is endemic to the Guatemalan central highlands in pine-oak forests, at elevations from 1,300–2,135 m (4,265–7,005 ft). A. daryi is large for its species group. Females may attain a total length (including tail) of 57 cm (22 in), and males may exceed 40 cm (16 in) in total length. Dary's burrowing snake is terrestrial, fossorial, and mainly nocturnal.
